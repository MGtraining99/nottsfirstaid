First Aid at Work

The comprehensive three-day workplace first aid qualification. Required in higher-risk environments and larger organisations — the gold standard for appointed first aiders.

Home›Courses›First Aid at Work

Who is this course for?

The First Aid at Work (FAW) qualification is the most comprehensive workplace certificate and is required in many environments. Suitable for:

  • Appointed first aiders in manufacturing, construction, engineering, warehousing and other higher-risk settings
  • Larger organisations requiring a more thorough level of first aid provision
  • Workplaces where the HSE risk assessment specifies FAW
  • Those seeking the most highly regarded workplace first aid qualification

What does the course cover?

The FAW course significantly expands on the EFAW, covering a much wider range of injuries and medical emergencies:

  • Role and responsibilities of the workplace first aider
  • Managing an unconscious casualty; CPR and AED
  • Choking — adults
  • Wounds, severe bleeding and shock
  • Burns and scalds
  • Bone, muscle and joint injuries — fractures, dislocations, sprains
  • Head, neck and spinal injuries
  • Chest injuries
  • Eye injuries
  • Sudden illness — heart attack, stroke, seizures, diabetes, asthma, anaphylaxis
  • Poisoning
  • First aid reporting and record keeping

Assessment

Continuous practical assessment across all three days plus an MCQ exam / questioning. Delegates must demonstrate competency in all required skills.

Accreditation

Accredited through FAIB. Regulated qualification through Highfield Qualifications also available.
