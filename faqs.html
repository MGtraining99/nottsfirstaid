Common Questions

Got a question? We've got answers.

Can't find what you need? Contact us directly and we'll be happy to help.

What is the difference between EFAW and FAW?▼
Emergency First Aid at Work (EFAW) is a one-day course covering core emergency skills, suitable for lower-risk workplaces. First Aid at Work (FAW) is a comprehensive three-day qualification covering a much wider range of injuries and illnesses, required in higher-risk environments. The appropriate course depends on your workplace risk assessment — we're happy to advise.
How do we decide which first aid course our business needs?▼
The HSE recommends completing a first aid needs assessment considering the number of employees, the nature of the work, workplace hazards and location. We can help you work through this — get in touch for free advice on which course is right for your business.
How long are first aid certificates valid?▼
All of our first aid certificates are valid for three years. We strongly recommend requalifying before expiry — if a certificate lapses, delegates must retake the full qualification rather than the shorter requalification course. We can send reminders ahead of renewal dates.
What areas do you cover for on-site training?▼
We deliver throughout Nottinghamshire and Derbyshire, including Nottingham, Derby, Mansfield, Newark, Chesterfield, Matlock, Long Eaton, Hucknall, Ilkeston and surrounding areas. If you're unsure whether we can reach your location, please get in touch.
What do we need to provide for on-site training?▼
We bring all training materials, CPR manikins and equipment. You need to provide a suitable room with enough floor space for practical activities, seating for all delegates, and ideally a table or desk area. Let us know your space and we'll confirm suitability.
What is the minimum number of delegates for on-site training?▼
We recommend a minimum of 4 delegates to make on-site delivery cost-effective. However, smaller groups can be accommodated — contact us for a tailored quote. Maximum group size is typically 12 to maintain training quality.
What is FAIB and are the qualifications recognised?▼
The First Aid Industry Body (FAIB) is the recognised awarding and accreditation body for first aid qualifications in the UK. FAIB-accredited certificates are widely accepted by employers and HSE-compliant. We also offer Highfield Qualifications (Ofqual-regulated) versions where a regulated certificate is required.
Are your qualifications accepted by Ofsted?▼
Yes. Our Paediatric First Aid qualification meets the EYFS statutory requirement. We recommend the Highfield-regulated version for Ofsted-registered settings — please mention this when booking so we issue the correct certificate.
Can training days be spread out rather than consecutive?▼
For multi-day courses we can discuss flexible scheduling. Days don't have to be consecutive but should be completed within a reasonable timeframe to maintain continuity. Contact us to arrange dates that suit your operation.
Does MG Training offer other compliance courses?▼
Yes — MG Training also delivers Licensing (APLH), Food Safety, HACCP, Health & Safety, and Education & Training qualifications. See our About Us page for details, or visit mgtraining.co.uk.
