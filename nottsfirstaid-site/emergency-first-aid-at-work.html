Emergency First Aid at Work

A one-day qualification covering the essential skills to manage first aid emergencies. The most widely held workplace first aid certificate — ideal for lower-risk environments.

Home›Courses›Emergency First Aid at Work

Who is this course for?

The Emergency First Aid at Work (EFAW) qualification is designed for any employee who may need to respond to a first aid emergency at work. It is particularly suited to:

  • Appointed first aiders in offices, retail, hospitality, and other lower-risk workplaces
  • Small businesses where a full FAW is not required by the risk assessment
  • Employees where the HSE needs assessment indicates EFAW is appropriate
  • Staff needing a refresh of emergency first aid skills

What does the course cover?

  • Role and responsibilities of the first aider
  • Managing an unconscious casualty and recovery position
  • Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) — adult
  • Use of an Automated External Defibrillator (AED)
  • Choking — adults
  • Bleeding and shock — recognition and management
  • Burns and scalds
  • Seizures
  • Minor injuries

Assessment

Assessment is continuous and practical throughout the course. Delegates are assessed on practical skill demonstrations and an MCQ exam / questioning.

Accreditation

Accredited through the First Aid Industry Body (FAIB). A regulated qualification through Highfield Qualifications is also available on request.
