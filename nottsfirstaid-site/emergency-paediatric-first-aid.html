Paediatric First Aid

Emergency Paediatric First Aid

A one-day paediatric qualification for those working with children in lower-risk settings — childminders, teaching assistants and after-school staff.

Home›Courses›Emergency Paediatric First Aid

Who is this course for?

The Emergency Paediatric First Aid (EPFA) qualification is designed for anyone working with or caring for babies and children who requires a paediatric-focused first aid certificate:

  • Registered childminders (one PFA certificate required per Ofsted)
  • Teaching assistants, early years support staff and after-school workers
  • Nannies, au pairs and private childcare workers
  • Play workers and youth workers

Note: Ofsted-registered nurseries and day care providers may require the full two-day Paediatric First Aid (PFA) qualification. Check your registration requirements before booking.

What does the course cover?

  • Role of the paediatric first aider
  • Managing an unresponsive baby or child
  • CPR for infants and children
  • Choking in babies and children
  • Shock — recognition and management
  • Bleeding and wounds
  • Burns and scalds
  • Febrile seizures
  • Common childhood medical conditions

Assessment

Continuous practical assessment throughout the day, including paediatric CPR and choking scenarios, plus an MCQ exam / questioning.

Accreditation

Accredited through FAIB. Regulated qualification through Highfield Qualifications also available.
