Blended Learning First Aid

Complete the theory online at your own pace, then attend a shorter practical classroom session at your premises. Same qualification, smarter delivery.

Home›Blended Learning
How It Works

Learn online first, then practise in person

Blended learning splits the qualification into an online theory element and a shorter practical classroom session. The e-learning must be completed before the classroom day.

1

Complete e-learning

Work through online modules at your own pace. Access provided when you confirm your booking.

2

Finish before class

All e-learning must be completed before the classroom day. Delegates who have not finished cannot take part.

3

Classroom at your site

Shorter on-site practical day focused on skills, scenarios and assessment. Same standard, less time on site.

Important

E-learning must be completed first

The online element is a mandatory part of the qualification. Delegates who arrive at the classroom session without completing the e-learning will be unable to participate and may lose their place.

E-learning access links and instructions are provided when you confirm your booking.

Same qualification. Smarter delivery.

Blended learning leads to exactly the same FAIB-accredited or Highfield-regulated certificate as the full classroom version. No difference in qualification, assessment standard or the certificate you receive.
